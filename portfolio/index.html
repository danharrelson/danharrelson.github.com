Hi, I'm Dan

Thanks for taking a peek at my portfolio. Hopefully these work samples give you a taste for who I am and what I do. There's much, much more and I'd be happy to fill in details and gaps; just let me know when you'd like to chat.

Throughout my career I have worked at the intersection of design & development. Starting early during my education in print design and throughout my career working in digital media, the interweaving of design and implementation always just felt right. I've been lucky enough to earn many unique opportunities to share this view with others through leadership, writing and speaking.

Over the last 20+ years I have designed interfaces, architected systems, built apps, lead teams and consulted with many, many fortune 500 clients. Why the variety? Because I like a challenge. Being pushed into new places is exciting; I want to always be learning. Maybe it's experimenting with new technology like machine learning. Maybe it's learning a technique for user research. Maybe it's finding out about a new culture. Maybe it's designing for a brand new device. I find that my best ideas emerge when collaborating with smart people, in respectful and creative environments.

Yell.com

I lead an in-depth user research program for Yell.com. As the primary resource to find small businesses in the UK, Yell wanted to offer affordable website creation service to these SMB's. We embarked on a 2 month research program. While still in San Francisco, I came up to speed on Yell's core business offering and the problem they wished to solve. This was followed by two weeks spent on-the-ground in the UK making visits to a dozen business owner's homes and offices. Each research participant walked through a series of activities intended to reveal how they market services and what they consider to be "great design". Findings from this ethnographic study were analyzed and ultimately I delivered a report including actionable design direction for Yell's offshore site builders.

smart.fm

I designed and developed a native iPhone application for smart.fm. Already popular online learning platform, smart.fm wanted to expand it's reach via mobile users. In just a couple of weeks our team defined the appropriate features for the mobile experience, and designed the look, feel and interaction of the app. I then went to work building the actual iPhone app. The app features communication with the smart.fm API (via JSON) to download a user's learning goals. Significant technical milestones of the app include asynchronous download of data, local storage of data objects for offline use, secure login and sign up of users and iPhone-specific animation. You can read a case study about the app design on the Adaptive Path blog.
